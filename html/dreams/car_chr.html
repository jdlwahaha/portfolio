Requirements | Toyota C-HR
I finally got a car that meet most of my criteria. It's about one year after I put down all the requirements for a car. It ticks most of the boxes and let's review!
Must have/be:
  • ✔ Two doors only
    It's actually a 5-door small crossover, but I like how the backseat handles are hidden on the top of the car.

    Sufficient of room in the backseats if I want to take more passengers. However, I'll likely keep the seats down because the truck space is quite small compare to what I'm used to.
  • ✔ Sharp appearance
    This is very subjective. Lots of people don't like the look, but I think it looks pretty sharp and feirce.
  • ✘ Dashcams
    I will have to install this myself. The dealer did offer, but the price was unreasonable.
  • ✔ Collision/Safety sensors
    This model has blind spot sensor which turns on the indicator on the side mirror. It has automatic breaks if it detects that the car is too close to the car in front of it. As a bonus point, it also has lane detection which beeps to notify that the car is too close to the lines.
  • ✔ Comfortable and ergonomic seats
    This is again a very subjective point. I'm not looking for message chair type of comfy, but good enough that it doesn't hurt my back for my daily commute.
  • ✔ Automatic
    This car is automatic only. It has three driving modes: Eco, Normal, and Sport.

    The driving experience is not that great. I find that there's a short delay between gasing, breaking, or turning when in Eco and Normal modes (very similar to newer version of Camry).

    Sport mode seems to be doing better, but the car does not preserves the Sport mode setting. It always reset to either Eco or Normal when you restart the car.
Optional:
  • ✘ No GPS
    It doesn't look like there's GPS, but there is an option for subscription. So maybe there is. I'm not sure about the geotracking of this car actually.
  • ✔ Not using premium gas
  • ✔ Heated front seat
    Bonus: Heated driving wheel too!!
  • ✘ Success yellow (not taxi)
